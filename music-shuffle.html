Weapons

Mjölnir

The hammer of Thor,the god of thunder.It is one of the most fearsome and powerful weapons in existence, capable of leveling mountains.The hammer was made by the dwarven brothers Eitri and Brokkr.

Gungnir

Odin’s own spear. It hit whatever you threw it at. It didn’t matter how strong or how skilled you were, whatever you threw Gungnir at, you hit.

Gram

The sword used to slay Fafnir the dragon. It was a pretty powerful weapon.

Dáinsleif

This was king Högni's sword.It gave wounds that never healed and could not be unsheathed without killing a man.

Monsters

Fenrir

Fenrir is a giant wolf who lurked in the background of Norse culture, waiting for the day when he would be set free to devour as many of the Norse gods as possible.

Huginn and Muninn

Huginn and Muninn are a pair of ravens who are enlisted in Odin’s service. The birds depart every morning at dawn to fly around the Norse world, Midgard. At dinner, they return to their perches on Odin’s shoulders and tell him what they have seen.

Valkyrie

Mortal woman who has had magnificent powers bestowed on her by Odin. They are responsible for watching over battlefields and collecting the best of the best warriors to make up Odin’s army, which he will unleash during Ragnarok.

Jormungand

In the depths of the ocean, a giant serpent named Jormungandr is biding his time. Prophecies tell us that he will emerge in Ragnarok to destroy the Nine Worlds of Norse mythology.

Ymir

He was first being. A giant created from drops of water that formed when the ice of Niflheim mixed with the heat of Muspelheim. Ymir was considered the father of all ice giants.

Nidhogg

Nidhogg is a ferocious dragon who gnaws at the roots of Yggdrasil, the tree which supports the nine worlds of Norse mythology. This power-hungry monster is sometimes referred to as “the Malice Striker,”He rules over dark criminals and is bent on destroying peace and virtue.

Sleipnir

Sleipnir is Odin’s valiant steed. He carries him on many quests, and he never fails to leave common men awestruck by his strength and speed.

Ratatoskr

Scurrying up and down the Norse tree of life is Ratatoskr, a squirrel with a taste for mischief. Ratatoskr may have big responsibilities as a messenger for the gods, but he doesn’t accept his duties meekly.
